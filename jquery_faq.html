Muir Woods, CA

  • Named after famed conservationist, John Muir.
  • Trees in the area are estimated to be 600-800 years old on average.
  • Contains the only old-growth coastal redwood forest in the Bay Area and one of the last on the planet.

Arches National Monument, UT

  • Has a 50-site campground (plus additional campgrounds in areas around the park).
  • Prior to becoming a national park, Arches was part of a large cattle ranch owned by a Civil War veteran.
  • Contains the densest concentration of natural stone arches in the world.

Denali National Park, AL

  • Glaciers cover a million acres, or a sixth of the park.
  • Earthquakes are frequent in the area. There are an estimated 600 seismic events greater than or equal to one magnitude per year.
  • The highest peak in North America, Denali, used to be known as Mt. McKinley, but its name was changed in 2016 to honor the indigenous peoples that had inhabited the area for so long.
